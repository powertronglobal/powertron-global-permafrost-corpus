The post DB temperature (74 vs. 74.3 deg. F) entering the coil was matched as close as possible to the 
baseline period. After matching this temperature the post readings were taken to ensure that the work the 
coil had to do was as similar to the baseline. Normalizing the post amperage was not necessary even though 
the entering condenser tower water was 5.14% cooler during the post measurements. The water flow of the 
condenser was adjusted so that the compressor discharge pressures would match.

and avoided energy reporting periods were not in accordance with design. This accounts for increased capacity over 
design as detailed in the calculated analysis of the measured and verified data for both the baseline and the avoided 
energy reporting periods. Please see the Conclusion/Notes at the end of the report for further details.
